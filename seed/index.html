What LIME Is

Two things, one name.

LIME is two things wearing one name. An inner discipline, and an outer tool. The inner one is called Fire Spirit — it's not a doctrine you join, it's the condition you're in before you touch a chart. The outer one is Rosie — four instruments that watch the market so you don't have to watch it with a hunter's eyes.

Here's the part most systems skip: the state you bring into the market is the actual variable. Not the signal. Not the setup. You. A calm operator running a good plan gets a different result than an anxious operator running the same plan — same tools, same market, different outcome, because the outcome was never only about the tools. Frequency, if that's your word for it. Peace, if that's yours. Same behavior, two names for it — steady instead of hunting, received instead of grabbed at.

That's why LIME doesn't start with the trade. It starts with the person placing it. Three returns in the morning, six at midday, nine at close — not a magic number, a rhythm that trains the reflex out of chasing and into watching. Ruth stayed when leaving was reasonable. Daniel read the room without flinching. Different centuries, same discipline: hold the line, move at the right moment, don't force the harvest.

Rosie is what that discipline looks like once it has hands. Four tools — Color Weather, Navigation, the Ark, Exhaustion — reading the market against itself continuously, so the practice runs even on the days your own attention doesn't. Approval-based. Nothing armed until you say so. Size matched to weather, never to hope.

We cannot and do not guarantee profits — no honest system can. What we can make is a calm account with a defined stop and an operator who isn't white-knuckling the screen. Loss prevention first, then profit. P&L is downstream of that. A steady person running a steady system — that's the whole offer.

Every symbol is judged relative to SPY — the whole market — never in isolation. A symbol that looks strong on its own chart but is weaker than the market is a lower-quality setup, no matter how green its own lights glow.

The Golden Rule
Equal to or stronger than SPY = favorable.
Weaker than SPY = downsize or skip.
Enforced across all four tools, not just one. Sail with the wind, not against it.

LIME Color Weather

Colors the chart like a weather map. Green seas = friendly, you can be a little bolder. Yellow = caution, smaller size. Red = storm, stay small or stay out. Match your size to the weather. Never fight red weather.

Navigation Sensor Array

Shows which parts of the market are strong and pulling the boat forward. Green, leading sectors = clear direction. Mixed or flat = no leader, wait. Red = wrong way, stand aside. The SPY check lives here — only take trades at least as strong as the market.

Captain's Plan · how big to be

Noal's Ark

The Ark is your safe plan. It tells you exactly how much of your account to use for this trip so you never risk too much. Green = safe size. Yellow = smaller than usual. Red = too risky, skip. Follow the Ark's size exactly — never go bigger, even when you feel excited.

Tiredness Gauge · is the move fresh

LIME Exhaustion

Tells you if price has been running hard and is getting tired, like a kid who played all day. Low and fresh = energy remains, good time to consider entry. Medium = tiring, trail the stop. High = exhausted, harvest profits or stay out. Enter fresh, trim tired.

Observe. Classify. Rank. Propose. Execute — only if armed. Rosie never reaches for the trade. She watches, sorts, ranks, and quietly suggests. The moment of action belongs to the operator alone.

Observe

Read the sky and the radar before anything else. Weather, sectors, and the SPY-relative check come first.

Classify

Sort each symbol into a single clear state. Probe, Armed, Track, or Rest. One posture at a time.

Rank

Line up setups by quality. Equal-or-stronger-than-SPY floats to the top. Weaker ones sink, no matter how they look alone.

Propose

Rosie offers a direction, a size from the Ark, and a pre-named exit. She never pulls the trigger herself.

Execute — only if armed

The operator intentionally arms the route. No arming, no trade. Harbor by 9:30. Stops on structure, immediately.

Rosie is built to protect family money first. Profit follows from discipline, not the other way around. The goal is never the big trade — it is an account that sleeps through the night.

No trade without a written reason, a target range, and a pre-named exit or trim.

A stop on the symbol's own structure, placed immediately after entry. No unprotected legs.

Pyramid base starts at half size, capped at 1.1x total. Additions only when leg rules are met.

Harbor by 9:30 AM and after any major move. When scattered, track — don't execute.
